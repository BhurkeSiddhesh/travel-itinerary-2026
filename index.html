11:00 AM

Namdroling Monastery — Golden Temple, Bylakuppe

📍 Maps

Park outside. Remove footwear at the gate. This is the largest Nyingma Buddhist settlement outside Tibet — 5,000+ Tibetan refugees settled here since the 1960s. Enter the Zangdog Palri Fo-Brang temple first: three golden Buddhas (the central one at 18 feet), 1,000 smaller figures, ceiling murals that took 10 years to complete.

Find a corner and sit quietly for 10 minutes. Monks do puja rounds throughout the day. Walk the mani prayer wheel corridor — spin each wheel clockwise with your right hand. The Tibetan Vajrayana tradition here absorbed significant Hindu tantric elements — the fierce protective deities at the entrance are identical in concept to the Durga forms in Hindu temples. This overlap is worth spending time with. Spend 1.5–2 hours here, not 30 minutes.

1:00 PM
Lunch

Tibetan Restaurants — Outside Monastery

📍 Maps

Several Tibetan restaurants line the street just outside. Order: Thupka (noodle broth — Tibet's version of ramen), Steamed momos (not fried), Butter tea if curious (salty, acquired taste). Honest food, under ₹200 a head.

A small tank with turtles and fish in the courtyard. This architectural fusion tells Coorg's complex history in one building.

Dubare Elephant Camp — Kaveri River

📍 Maps

Talacauvery — Source of the Kaveri, 1276m

📍 Maps

A small sacred kund (tank) marks the exact spring. The Kaveri flows 800 km from this quiet hilltop to the Bay of Bengal — feeding 80 million people, irrigating the delta rice bowl, powering the Kabini reservoir you'll see in Nagarhole. Climb the 50 steps to the Brahmagiri viewpoint: 360° of the Coorg ranges. On a clear day, you can see the Western Ghats all the way to Kerala. Deeply underrated spot. 45 minutes.

Highfield Tea Factory — Working Factory

📍 Maps
